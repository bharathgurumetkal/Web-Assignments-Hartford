The term "Hartford" generally refers to either Hartford, Connecticut, the capital city known as the "Insurance Capital of the World,"or The Hartford Financial Services Group, Inc., the major insurance and investment company headquartered there. Hartford, Connecticut, is among the oldest cities in the United States, founded in 1635, and is historically significant as the place where the Fundamental Orders of Connecticut, a framework for early self-government, were inspired. Once the wealthiest U.S. city following the Civil War, it later faced economic challenges but remains the core of a metropolitan area with over a million residents. The city earned its "Insurance Capital" nickname due to the high concentration of insurance company headquarters, which form the region's major industry. It is home to cultural landmarks like the Wadsworth Atheneum, the nation's second oldest public art museum, and the preserved homes of literary figures such as Mark Twain and Harriet Beecher Stowe.
